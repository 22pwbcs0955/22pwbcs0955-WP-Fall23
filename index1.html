Featured Travel Story

Gwanghwamun Square, located in the heart of Seoul, is a vibrant public space that beautifully blends history and modernity. As you step into the square, the iconic Gwanghwamun Gate stands tall, welcoming you to a journey through time.The square is surrounded by historical landmarks, including the statue of King Sejong the Great and Admiral Yi Sun-sin. These statues pay homage to South Korea's rich cultural heritage and its historical figures who played pivotal roles in shaping the nation.As you stroll through the square, you'll notice the seamless integration of traditional and contemporary elements. The open space is often bustling with locals and tourists, creating a lively atmosphere. Cafes and shops line the perimeter, offering the perfect spots to relax and take in the surroundings.>Don't forget to capture the breathtaking view of Gyeongbokgung Palace in the background. The juxtaposition of ancient palaces against the backdrop of a modern cityscape creates a captivating scene that's perfect for photography enthusiasts.Whether you're interested in history, architecture, or simply want to soak in the vibrant energy of Seoul, Gwanghwamun Square is a must-visit destination. It's a place where the past meets the present, and every corner tells a story
